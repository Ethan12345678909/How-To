How to Drive Manual

Driving manual helps engage the driver and give them a connection to the car. Driving manual may seem difficult but with this "how-to" guide, you'll be able to get out on the road in your manual and know all the beginners tips you'll need. Impress everyone with your smooth accelerations and handbrake turns at that next car show. Drive stick, and your passenger will instantly fall in love with you.

Step 1

Turn car on. Make sure car is in neutral (when you can freely move ths shifter from side to side). Press foot on clutch. And start the vehicle by pressing the button or turning the key.
Step 2

Your car is on! To shift into first gear to get going, keep one foot on the clutch still, and one foot on the brake. Move the shifter into the 1st slot. A gear pattern should be shown on or near the shifter to help locate first.
Step 3

Your car is in first gear. Make sure your parking brake is off. You can do this by pressing the botton towards the front and moving it down. Then releasing the button. If you take your foot off the brake, the car will creep.
Step 4

To accelerate. Take your right foot off the brake and onto the accelerator. Like a normal automatic. But keep the clutch in. As you press the accelerator, let off the clutch as you go. Be slow, do not release the clutch right away. You should feel the car "jump" forward, this lets you know to take the clutch off completely.
Step 5

Your car should accelerate more traditionally now. Next, lets change gears! But first, make sure you have sufficient revs to change up. DO NOT change up if you feel the engine revs "jumping around and juttering." Give the engine more revs.
Step 6

To change up, take your foot off the acclerator. As you do this, apply the clutch in a progressive manner, in relation to how fast you let off the accelerator. Now the clutch is in. Slot the gear lever into the 2nd position. This may vary from car to car. Now, do the opposite. Let off the clutch and let into the accelerator in a progressive manner.
Step 7

You just changed gears! Now you are able to change up into any gear you want. Let's learn how to downshift into a lower gear.
Step 8

To change down, do the same thing as when changing up. Let in clutch, let off accelerator and move the shifter into a lower gear. You are able to skip gears when changing up or down. For example, changing from 3rd to 5th in one shift.
Step 9

Let's stop! Apply brakes like a traditional car, change down appropriately. Before you stop, around 10 mph, let in clutch all the way, you should still apply brakes to stop fully. Now you are in the same position as when you set off. One foot on clutch and one foot on brake, and in first.
Step 10

To reverse, do the same thing just move the gear lever into reverse, this is clearly located on your shifter pattern. You can find reverse in a corner or "out" of the normal gear area.
Step 11

To park your car, with the clutch in, move the gear lever into the middle, where you can freely move it from side to side. Make sure to apply the brakes until the handbrake is on. You can achieve this by pulling it up. Do not press the button, just pull it up. You now parked your car and can turn it off.
Step 12

What should I do if I stall? You stalled if you let off the clutch too early and are not going fast enough. Don't worry! Stop the car and keep the clutch in, start it again and continue. NOTE: stalling repeatedly will cause damage to the clutch, so take caution and go slow.
Driving manual takes time and patience. You may not get it on the first try. Don't sweat it. Start slow and practice!
